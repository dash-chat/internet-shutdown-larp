“Only a handful of relief stations survived, each with its own short-range radio post: the firefighters, the hospital, the uplink to the outside world, the long-range link to Riverside next door — and this town hall. They cannot talk to each other any more. Their messages must now travel in your pockets.”

“Will you help us carry them?”

📋 Your instructions

  1. Open Dash Chat and add your partner as a contact (scan each other’s QR code).
  2. Scan the four QR posters on the wall around you: Cindy the firefighter, James the nurse, Marta the journalist, and Aunt Anna in Riverside, the town next door.
  3. Create a group with your partner and all four of them.
  4. Split up: one of you on each side of the fire. Only this town hall square — the firebreak — is shared ground.
  5. Every station broadcasts its own Wi-Fi network, and they all share the same name pattern: firefighters-larp, hospital-larp, nearby-town-radio-link-larp, … That’s how you’ll recognize them.
  6. The stations’ emergency radios are weak: you must stand very close to a station before your phone can connect to its network.
  7. Walk into a station’s Wi-Fi to pick up its messages and to deliver the ones you carry. Deliver each message to the station it asks for.
  8. Messages for the far side of the fire: bring them here — the town hall notice board keeps them until your partner passes by.
  9. Keep mobile data OFF — the town’s networks are down. Wi-Fi only.
  10. Forget every other Wi-Fi network saved on your phone (home, work, hotspots…). Otherwise your phone will sneak back onto them instead of staying with the stations.

Checking the notice board…

This network has no internet. You can safely dismiss any sign-in screen and keep using the town network.
